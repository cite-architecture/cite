Extracting parts of CTS URNs

From a URN like urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.msA:2.75, we can extract:

  • the identifer for its CTS namespace, greekLit
  • its text group identifier, either with (greekLit:tlg0012) or without (tlg0012) namespace qualifier
  • its work identifier, either with (greekLit:tlg001) or without (tlg001) namespace qualifier
  • its version identifier, either with (greekLit:msA) or without (msA) namespace qualifier
  • the identifier for its passage node, 2.75

For a URN with an exemplar-level identifier, like urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.msA.lex:1.1.1, we can extract:

  • its text group identifier, either with (greekLit:tlg0012) or without (tlg0012) namespace qualifier
  • its work identifier, either with (greekLit:tlg001) or without (tlg001) namespace qualifier
  • its version identifier, either with (greekLit:msA) or without (msA) namespace qualifier
  • And its exemplar identifier, either with (greekLit:lex) or without (lex) namespace qualifier

For a URN citing a range, like urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.msA:1.1-2.75, we can also extract:

  • the first node of the range, 1.1
  • the last node of the range, 2.75

For a URN with a subreference like urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.msA:1.1@Μῆνιν, we can extract:

  • the whole passage component as submitted to the constructor, 1.1@Μῆνιν
  • the subreference, Μῆνιν, and its implicit index 1.

The semantically equivalent URN urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.msA:1.1@Μῆνιν[1] yields the results:

  • passage component, 1.1@Μῆνιν[1]
  • subreference, Μῆνιν, and explicit index 1.

For a range URN with subreferences like urn:cts:greekLit:tlg0012.tlg001.msA:1.1@Μῆνιν[1]-1.2@οὐλομένην, we can also extract:

  • the first subreference, Μῆνιν and its (explicit) index 1
  • the second subreference, οὐλομένην and its (implicit) index 1
